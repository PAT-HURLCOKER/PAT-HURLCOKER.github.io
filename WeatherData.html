The effort today is to compare the results of two data sets (a data source provided 1/5/2017 and the project results of 8/6/2018). All source data and visualizations are provided for reference.

Our analysis was to visualize and understand weather and its relationship as you get closer to the equator. We used Python/Pandas with Matplotlib componetns to plot the data. Our data source was a randomized dataset of at least 500 world cities using an OpenWeatherMap API call.

Our objective is to build a series of scatter plots to showcase the following relationships:

  • Cloudiness (%) vs. Latitude
  • Humidity (%) vs. Latitude
  • Temperature (F) vs. Latitude
  • Wind Speed (mph) vs. Latitude

Our project used a simple [Python library] , and the [OpenWeatherMap API]. We created a representative model of weather across world cities.


Our Considerations: The [geographic coordinate system] was reviewed prior to making any assessments in the final analysis. (http://desktop.arcgis.com/en/arcmap/10.3/guide-books/map-projections/about-geographic-coordinate-systems.htm).
